What is MUN?

Model United Nations (MUN) is a simulation of real UN conferences where students take on the roles of diplomats to debate pressing global issues. Open to grades 7–12, our conference will be held at The International School of Choueifat, 6 October. Beyond politics, MUN is about building leadership, critical thinking, and teamwork in a fun yet challenging environment. At ISC 6 October, we strive to create a supportive space where both delegates and organizers grow together. This year’s MUN councils include the ICJ, UNODC, Security Council, General Assembly (Junior council), and UNICEF (Junior council). This year's theme is Equity, Empathy, and Global Responsibility

Our Councils

UNSC

United Nations Security Council: Focuses on maintaining international peace and security by addressing conflicts, sanctions, and peacekeeping operations.

DISEC

Disarmament and International Security Committee: Addresses global challenges related to disarmament, arms control, and international security threats.

UNODC

The United Nations Office on Drugs and Crime:a UN body dedicated to tackling illicit drugs, organized crime, corruption, and terrorism.

ICJ

International Court of Justice: The UN’s judicial arm, where real-life legal disputes between countries are debated, judged, and settled. Delegates act as advocates, judges, or justices.

UNGA-Junior Council

United Nations General Assembly: A platform for all 193 member states to discuss and collaborate on global issues ranging from climate change to international law.

UNICEF-Junior Council

United Nations International Children's Emergency Fund: Advocates for the rights and wellbeing of children, focusing on education, nutrition, and child protection worldwide.
